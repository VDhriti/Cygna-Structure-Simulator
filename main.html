Central Shaft

Every Settlement traditionally begins with a Central Shaft which acts as a huge travel hub, an axis of rotation for other volumes etc. The material of this unpressurized volume majorly influences cost, strength and damage protection of the structure.

One of the most important factors determining transportation traffic per channel and structural integrity. Angles might play a role in this choice.

Storage and Industries

Storage and Industries can majorly either be done under the 'floor' of the residential volumes or in a seperate smaller non pressurized, non rotating volume. Determining factors are portholes for residents, amount of storage required, type of settlement, isolability (potential isolated volumes should have food, water, air, waste management...), contingency (safety) etc.

Recreation

Mostly Depends on the purpose of the settlemnt and potentially the type of people who will be living there. Accesibility from Residential Volumes, presence of Variable artificial gravity values are a few major determinig factors.
